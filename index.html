Research Interests

I'm broadly interested in applying formal methods and machine learning technologies to motion planning algorithms for autonomous vehicles to increase their safety, liability, and efficiency. Some of my current research directions are:

Rule-Compliant Trajectory Repairing: it is computationally nontrivial to ensure the compliance of real-time motion planning with all traffic rule constraints

Criticality Measures for Autonomous Driving: the assumptions made for simply computed criticality assessments often do not hold, specifically when a longer prediction horizon is required

Game-Theoretic Motion Planning: we seek to enable an autonomous vehicle to interact with other vehicles, both human-driven and autonomous, while reasoning about other vehiclesâ€™ intentions and responses
